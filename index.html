Spiny Bush Viper

only growing up to 29 inches for males and 23 inches for females. these venomous reptiles are native to Africa. Males have a short snout. The eyes are large and round. They are known for there big scales that give a shaggy look to the scales. The scales around the head and neck are the longest, Spiny bush vipers vary in color . The males are long and skinny compared to the females.Their tails are strong . which helps them to crawl, climb, and hang upside down.

Location

they can be found in Central and East Africa: northern and eastern DR Congo, southwestern Uganda, west Kenya, and northwestern Tanzania

Venom

Not much is known about their venom except that it is mainly neurotoxic. Toxicity of a individual within the same species can vary greatly based on several factors, including geographical region. Even the weather and altitude can influence toxicity A bite can be fatal to humans without access to proper first aid and antivenom treatment. bites are uncommon.

Common Names

Rough-scaled bush viper, hairy bush viper, rough-scaled tree viper, African hairy bush viper, hairy viper.

Reproduction and Offspring

"Mating season for spiny bush vipers occurs during the rainy season between the end of summer and October. They reach sexual maturity between 2 and 3 years of age. Females are ovoviviparous, meaning they give birth to live young. After mating, females carry their fertilized eggs in their bodies for 6 to 7 months before giving birth to 9 to 12 young at a time in March or April. These young are about 6 inches in total length and are dark green with wavy stripes. They attain their adult coloration after 3 to 4 months. Due to their remote location from humans, scientists do not know their life span in the wild, but these creatures can live more than 12 years in captivity."

Diet and Behavior

These snakes eat small mammals, birds, lizards, and frogs. They hunt mostly in trees but can hunt for mammal prey on the ground. They ambush their prey by hanging from trees or hiding in foliage and curling up into an S-shape before lunging at prey, killing them with their venom. Spiny bush vipers are nocturnal creatures, spending the daytime basking on top of flowers in small trees about 10 feet off the ground.
